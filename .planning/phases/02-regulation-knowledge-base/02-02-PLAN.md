**Header:** "# COPPA -- Quick Reference" with "Last verified: March 2026" and note to consult FTC website for current rule text.

**Overview:** Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (1998), FTC Rule (2000, amended 2013, further amended January 2025). Protects children under 13 online. Applies to operators of commercial websites/online services directed to children or with actual knowledge of child users.

**Key Provisions:**
- Scope: operators of commercial websites/online services directed to children under 13, or with actual knowledge of collecting from children under 13
- Personal information definition (expanded in 2025: now includes biometrics, government IDs)
- Verifiable parental consent (VPC) required before collection -- methods include signed consent forms, credit card verification, video call, knowledge-based authentication, government ID verification
- 2025 rule amendments: separate consent for third-party disclosures (can no longer bundle), data retention limits (only as long as reasonably necessary), expanded PI definition, safe harbor transparency requirements

**Operator Obligations:**
- Direct notice to parents before collection
- Verifiable parental consent
- Data minimization: collect only what is reasonably necessary for the child's participation
- Data security: reasonable procedures to protect PI
- Deletion upon parent request
- Right to refuse further collection
- 2025 addition: data retention limits with mandatory deletion when no longer needed

**Safe Harbor Programs:**
- FTC-approved self-regulatory programs
- 2025 update: programs must publicly disclose membership; revised guidelines due October 2025
- Provides safe harbor from FTC enforcement if program guidelines followed

**Enforcement and Penalties:**
- Enforced by FTC
- Civil penalties per violation (adjusted for inflation -- currently up to ~$53,000+ per violation)
- No private right of action under COPPA
- FTC can seek injunctive relief and monetary penalties

**Key Enforcement Milestones (3-5):**
- Epic Games (Fortnite) $275M (2022) -- largest COPPA penalty ever, dark patterns and notification failures
- Disney $10M (2025) -- YouTube child-directed content, data collection through ads
- Apitor Technology $500K suspended (2024) -- geolocation collection via Chinese third party
- Musical.ly (TikTok) $5.7M (2019) -- collecting children's PI without parental consent
- Google/YouTube $170M (2019) -- tracking children on child-directed channels

**Recent Developments:**
- January 16, 2025: FTC finalized COPPA Rule amendments
- June 23, 2025: Amendments effective
- April 22, 2026: Compliance deadline for existing operators
- Expanded PI definition: biometric identifiers, government-issued IDs now covered
- Third-party disclosure requires separate, specific consent
- Safe harbor transparency reforms

**Compliance Essentials (5-7 items):**
1. Age gate or actual knowledge mechanism operational
2. Verifiable parental consent obtained before ANY collection of PI from children under 13
3. Direct notice to parents includes all required disclosures
4. Separate consent for third-party disclosures (2025 requirement)
5. Data retention limited to reasonable necessity; deletion when no longer needed
6. Data security measures reasonable and appropriate
7. Safe harbor program membership current and publicly disclosed (if participating)

Stand alone without FPF references.

**Header:** "# HIPAA -- Quick Reference" with "Last verified: March 2026" and note to consult HHS website for current guidance.

**Overview:** Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (1996), Privacy Rule effective April 14, 2003. Protects individually identifiable health information (PHI) held by covered entities and their business associates. Sector-specific -- applies to healthcare, not all industries.

**Key Provisions:**
- Protected Health Information (PHI): individually identifiable health information transmitted or maintained by a covered entity or business associate, in any form
- Covered entities: health plans, healthcare clearinghouses, healthcare providers who conduct standard electronic transactions
- Business associates: persons/entities performing functions on behalf of covered entities involving PHI access
- Minimum necessary standard: use, disclose, and request only the minimum PHI necessary for the intended purpose
- Treatment, Payment, Operations (TPO) exception: PHI can be used/disclosed for TPO without individual authorization
- De-identification: Safe Harbor method (remove 18 identifiers) and Expert Determination method

**Patient Rights:**
- Access to PHI (with 30-day response deadline)
- Amendment of PHI
- Accounting of disclosures
- Request restrictions on uses/disclosures
- Confidential communications
- Notice of Privacy Practices
- Right to file complaint with HHS

**Business Associate Requirements:**
- Business Associate Agreement (BAA) required before sharing PHI
- BAA must specify permitted uses, safeguards, breach notification obligations
- Business associates directly liable under HITECH Act

**Enforcement and Penalties:**
- Enforced by HHS Office for Civil Rights (OCR)
- Tiered penalty structure (2026 inflation-adjusted):
  - Tier 1 (did not know): $141-$71,162 per violation
  - Tier 2 (reasonable cause): $1,424-$71,162 per violation
  - Tier 3 (willful neglect, corrected): $14,232-$71,162 per violation
  - Tier 4 (willful neglect, not corrected): $71,162 per violation
  - Annual caps up to $2.19M per violation category
- Criminal penalties for knowing violations: fines up to $250K and/or imprisonment

**Key Enforcement Milestones (3-5):**
- Anthem $16M (2018) -- largest HIPAA settlement historically, data breach affecting 78.8M individuals
- Montefiore Medical Center $4.75M (2024) -- insider threat, employee stole PHI
- Solara Medical $3M (2025) -- phishing attack, inadequate security measures
- Banner Health $1.25M (2023) -- failure to conduct enterprise-wide risk analysis
- OCR Risk Analysis Initiative launched fall 2024 -- targeting entities without documented risk analyses

**Recent Developments:**
- Reproductive health privacy amendments: finalized April 2024, partially vacated by court order June 2025 -- uncertain status
- Security Rule modernization: proposed December 27, 2024; expected finalization ~May 2026; would require encryption, MFA, network segmentation
- OCR Risk Analysis Initiative: enforcement sweep targeting entities that never conducted a risk analysis
- Ongoing emphasis on right of access enforcement

**Compliance Essentials (6-8 items):**
1. Risk analysis conducted and documented (enterprise-wide, not just IT)
2. Notice of Privacy Practices current and available to all patients
3. BAAs in place with every business associate before PHI sharing
4. Minimum necessary standard applied to all uses and disclosures
5. Workforce training on PHI handling completed and documented
6. Breach notification procedures: individual notification within 60 days, HHS notification, media notification for 500+ breaches
7. Patient rights processes operational (access, amendment, accounting)
8. Policies and procedures documented and retained for 6 years

**Header:** "# FERPA -- Quick Reference" with "Last verified: March 2026" and note to consult DOE Student Privacy website (studentprivacy.ed.gov).

**Overview:** Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (1974). Protects the privacy of student education records at educational agencies/institutions receiving federal education funding. Applies to virtually all public schools and most postsecondary institutions. Rights transfer from parents to students at age 18 or when enrolled in postsecondary education ("eligible students").

**Key Provisions:**
- Education records: records directly related to a student, maintained by an educational agency/institution or a party acting for it
- Exclusions: sole possession notes, law enforcement records, employment records, treatment records, alumni records after attendance
- Directory information: name, address, phone, email, dates of attendance, degrees, honors, enrollment status -- may be disclosed without consent if proper notice and opt-out period provided
- Prior written consent required before disclosing education records (from parent or eligible student)

**Consent Exceptions (key enumerated exceptions):**
- School officials with legitimate educational interest (including ed-tech vendors designated as school officials)
- Transfer to another school where student seeks enrollment
- Financial aid purposes
- Accrediting organizations
- State/federal education authorities for audits and evaluations
- Health or safety emergency
- Judicial order or subpoena
- Directory information (with proper notice)
- Victims of crimes of violence (outcome only)

**Institutional Obligations:**
- Annual FERPA notification to parents/eligible students
- Process for inspecting and reviewing education records
- Process for requesting amendment of inaccurate records
- Policies on directory information disclosure with opt-out mechanism
- Maintain record of each disclosure of education records

**Enforcement:**
- Enforced by Family Policy Compliance Office (FPCO) within DOE
- NO monetary penalties -- enforcement mechanism is withdrawal of federal education funding
- Complaint-driven process (individuals file complaints with FPCO)
- In practice, enforcement has historically been rare and focused on compliance letters

**Key Issues and Challenges (in lieu of traditional enforcement milestones since FERPA lacks monetary penalties):**
- Ed-tech vendor management: vendors with access to education records must be designated as "school officials" with legitimate educational interest via contractual provisions
- Cybersecurity: FERPA does not mandate specific security measures, creating gaps as schools face increasing breaches
- AI in education: emerging questions about whether AI systems processing student data create new education records
- April 2025 DOE Dear Colleague letter on gender support plans and education records
- De-identification: FERPA allows disclosure of de-identified records but lacks Safe Harbor equivalent like HIPAA

**Recent Developments:**
- NPRM targeted for January 2026 to clarify education record definitions and update nonconsensual disclosure provisions
- Growing tension between ed-tech integration and FERPA compliance
- Cybersecurity incidents at schools raising questions about FERPA's adequacy for modern threats

**Compliance Essentials (5-6 items):**
1. Annual FERPA notification provided to parents and eligible students
2. Written consent obtained before disclosing education records (or exception documented)
3. Ed-tech vendor contracts include "school official" designation with legitimate educational interest scope, direct control requirements
4. Directory information policy in place with annual notice and opt-out period
5. Record of disclosures maintained for each education record
6. Inspection and amendment procedures documented and communicated

Stand alone without FPF references.
