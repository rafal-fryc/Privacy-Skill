**Header:** "# GDPR -- Quick Reference" with blockquote "Last verified: March 2026" and note to consult EDPB and national DPA websites for current enforcement.

**Overview (1-2 paragraphs):** Regulation (EU) 2016/679, adopted April 2016, enforceable May 25 2018, applies to all processing of EU/EEA residents' personal data regardless of processor location, extraterritorial reach.

**Key Provisions (practitioner-level detail):**
- All 6 lawful bases with 1-sentence descriptions each (consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public interest, legitimate interest)
- Data protection principles (Article 5): lawfulness/fairness/transparency, purpose limitation, data minimization, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity/confidentiality, accountability
- Personal data definition and special categories (Article 9)
- Consent requirements (freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous; clear affirmative action; withdrawal as easy as giving)

**Data Subject Rights (all 8 individually listed with scope):**
Access (Art 15), Rectification (Art 16), Erasure/right to be forgotten (Art 17), Restriction (Art 18), Portability (Art 20), Object (Art 21), Automated decision-making objection (Art 22), Withdraw consent

**Obligations and Requirements:**
- DPO requirements (when mandatory: public authority, large-scale special categories, large-scale systematic monitoring)
- Records of processing (Article 30)
- DPIA requirements (Article 35): when processing likely to result in high risk
- Data protection by design and by default (Article 25)
- Data processing agreements with processors (Article 28)
- Breach notification: 72 hours to supervisory authority, undue delay to data subjects when high risk

**International Transfers (Chapter V):**
- Adequacy decisions, SCCs (updated June 2021), BCRs
- EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework (July 2023 adequacy decision)
- Schrems II impact (Privacy Shield invalidated July 2020)

**Enforcement and Penalties:**
- Enforcement by national DPAs, EDPB for cross-border consistency
- Two-tier penalties: up to EUR 20M or 4% global annual turnover for substantive violations; EUR 10M or 2% for procedural
- One-stop-shop mechanism for cross-border processing
- Cumulative fines: EUR 7.1B+ since enforcement began

**Key Enforcement Milestones (3-5 landmark cases):**
- Meta EUR 1.2B (2023) -- international transfers to US, largest GDPR fine
- TikTok EUR 530M (2025) -- China data transfers
- Amazon EUR 746M (2021) -- targeted advertising, Luxembourg DPA
- Meta EUR 479M (2023) -- consent manipulation for behavioral advertising
- Google EUR 100M+ (multiple, CNIL) -- cookie consent violations

**Recent Developments:**
- 2026 EDPB Coordinated Enforcement Framework targeting transparency (Articles 12-14)
- Ongoing enforcement trend toward cross-border consistency
- DPF adequacy under periodic review

**Compliance Essentials (8 items):**
1. Lawful basis identified for every processing activity before processing begins
2. Privacy notice provided at/before data collection covering Articles 13/14
3. Data subject rights processes operational for all 8 rights with 30-day response deadline
4. DPO appointed if required
5. Records of processing activities maintained (Article 30)
6. Breach notification process: 72 hours to supervisory authority
7. International transfer mechanisms in place for any transfer outside EEA
8. Data processing agreements with all processors (Article 28)

Do NOT reproduce full statutory text. Include specific thresholds, dates, and amounts that practitioners need to recall or verify. The file must be usable without loading fpf-reference.md -- no references to FPF content.

**Header:** "# CCPA/CPRA -- Quick Reference" with "Last verified: March 2026" and note to consult CPPA website for current regulations.

**Overview:** CCPA (2018, effective Jan 1 2020), CPRA amendment (effective Jan 1 2023), California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA) as dedicated enforcement body. Applies to for-profit businesses meeting thresholds (annual revenue $25M+, 100K+ consumers/households, 50%+ revenue from selling PI). Note that major regulatory amendments took effect January 1, 2026.

**Key Provisions:**
- Scope and thresholds for covered businesses
- Personal information definition (broad, includes inferences)
- Sensitive personal information category (CPRA addition) with right to limit use
- Service provider / contractor / third party distinctions
- Opt-out model (not opt-in like GDPR)
- Global Privacy Control (GPC) -- must be honored as opt-out signal
- 2026 amendments: consent mechanisms, youth as sensitive PI category, data breach timelines

**Consumer Rights (complete list):**
- Right to know (categories and specific pieces)
- Right to delete
- Right to opt-out of sale/sharing
- Right to correct
- Right to limit use of sensitive PI
- Right to non-discrimination
- Response deadlines: 45 days (extendable to 90)

**Business Obligations:**
- Privacy policy requirements (updated annually)
- "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link
- Service provider written contracts with limited use restrictions
- Data minimization (CPRA addition)
- Risk assessments for certain processing
- Upcoming: ADMT requirements (Jan 2027), cybersecurity audits (phased 2027-2030)

**Enforcement and Penalties:**
- Dual enforcement: California AG + CPPA
- Civil penalties: $7,500 per intentional violation, $2,500 per unintentional
- Private right of action limited to data breach claims ($100-$750 per consumer per incident)
- No cure period for CPPA enforcement actions (removed by CPRA)

**Key Enforcement Milestones (3-5):**
- Sephora $1.2M (2022) -- first major CCPA enforcement, sale of PI through cookies
- Healthline $1.55M (2025) -- AG's largest privacy settlement
- Tractor Supply $1.35M (2025) -- CPPA's largest enforcement action to date
- DoorDash settlement (2024) -- sale of PI without consent
- Include one more if appropriate from research

**Recent Developments:**
- January 1, 2026 regulatory amendments (consent, youth privacy, breach timelines)
- ADMT regulations expected January 2027
- Risk assessment and cybersecurity audit requirements phased 2027-2030
- CPPA increasing enforcement cadence

**Compliance Essentials (6-8 items):**
1. Privacy policy current and updated annually with all required disclosures
2. "Do Not Sell or Share" link operational; GPC honored
3. Consumer rights request process operational (45-day response)
4. Service provider contracts in place with use restrictions
5. Sensitive PI processing limited unless consumer opts in
6. Data minimization: collect only what is reasonably necessary
7. Risk assessments conducted for high-risk processing (when regulations finalized)
8. Prepare for ADMT requirements (Jan 2027)

Same quality standards as Task 1. Stand alone without FPF references.
