# FPF Reference — Knowledge Base and Navigation Guide

This file contains the complete FPF knowledge base for the `/privacy` skill. Load this file when queries relate to FPF programs, publications, issue areas, specialized tools, or any privacy topic where FPF has relevant coverage.

> **Note:** Resource counts are approximate as of March 2026. FPF publishes new materials regularly; counts reflect a point-in-time snapshot, not a live tally. All FPF resources are Creative Commons licensed.

## 1. FPF Organization Overview

The **Future of Privacy Forum (FPF)** is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization advancing principled data protection, responsible AI governance, and digital privacy through research, publications, and stakeholder engagement.

- **Founded:** 2008
- **Offices:** Washington DC (headquarters), Brussels, Singapore
- **Website:** [https://fpf.org](https://fpf.org)
- **Focus:** Articles, publications, research reports, and policy analysis. FPF produces substantive analysis across 20+ privacy issue areas, translating complex regulatory and technical developments into actionable guidance for practitioners.
- **Scale:** 407+ publicly available resources across 20+ issue areas
- **License:** All public resources are Creative Commons licensed, enabling broad use and citation
- **Approach:** Multi-stakeholder, pragmatic, rights-balancing. FPF engages industry, regulators, civil society, and academia to develop privacy frameworks that are both principled and implementable.

## 2. Publication Types

FPF produces six distinct types of publications, each serving a different purpose and audience:

### Reports
Comprehensive research documents providing multi-page analysis of privacy topics. Reports are often co-authored with partner organizations, academic institutions, or industry collaborators. They represent FPF's most in-depth analytical work and typically include methodology, findings, and recommendations.
- **Typical length:** 15-80+ pages
- **Use case:** Deep policy analysis, regulatory impact assessment, technology evaluation
- **URL pattern:** Linked from issue area pages; PDFs hosted at `fpf.org/wp-content/uploads/`

### White Papers
Policy-focused analysis targeting specific regulatory or technical questions. Shorter and more focused than reports, white papers address a particular issue or proposed regulation with a clear analytical framework.
- **Typical length:** 5-20 pages
- **Use case:** Targeted policy recommendations, regulatory comment support, framework proposals
- **URL pattern:** PDFs hosted at `fpf.org/wp-content/uploads/`

### Filings and Comments
Formal regulatory submissions representing FPF's official positions on proposed regulations, FTC proceedings, state legislation, and international regulatory consultations. These document FPF's analytical perspective on active regulatory questions.
- **Use case:** Understanding FPF's regulatory positions, tracking proposed rule analysis
- **URL pattern:** Announcements at `fpf.org/blog/`; linked PDFs at `fpf.org/wp-content/uploads/`

### Infographics
Visual summaries of complex privacy topics. Often one-page or two-page visual guides to regulations, frameworks, compliance timelines, or concept explanations. Designed for quick reference and sharing.
- **Use case:** Quick reference, training materials, executive briefings
- **URL pattern:** PDFs hosted at `fpf.org/wp-content/uploads/`

### Blog Posts
Timely analysis and commentary published on FPF's blog. The primary channel for rapid response to privacy developments including new legislation, enforcement actions, regulatory guidance, and emerging technology issues.
- **Use case:** Current analysis, development tracking, expert commentary
- **URL pattern:** `fpf.org/blog/[post-slug]/`

### Videos
Presentations, panel discussions, and educational content from FPF events, programs, and webinars. Includes recorded conference sessions, expert interviews, and instructional content.
- **Use case:** Expert perspectives, event content, educational material
- **URL pattern:** FPF's YouTube channel; linked from event pages on fpf.org

## 3. Issue Areas

FPF organizes its work across 20+ distinct issue areas. Each area has a dedicated page on fpf.org with all related publications, tools, and analysis.

### U.S. Legislation
FPF tracks comprehensive privacy legislation across 50+ states, maintaining detailed analysis of enacted and proposed laws. Key resources include state law comparisons, enforcement analysis, legislative trend reports, and the Key Dates for State Privacy Laws tracker covering enforcement dates, cure periods, and opt-out signal deadlines. With 79+ resources, this is FPF's most extensively covered area.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/us-legislation/](https://fpf.org/issue/us-legislation/)
- **Key tool:** Key Dates for State Privacy Laws tracker
- **Resource types:** State law comparisons, enforcement timelines, legislative analyses, compliance guides

### Global Data Protection
FPF provides cross-jurisdictional analysis of data protection frameworks worldwide, covering GDPR, LGPD (Brazil), POPIA (South Africa), PIPL (China), UK GDPR, PIPEDA (Canada), PDPA (Singapore/Thailand), and the Australian Privacy Act. Resources focus on comparative analysis, data transfer mechanisms, adequacy decisions, and implementation guidance for multinational organizations. Approximately 65+ resources.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/global-data-protection/](https://fpf.org/issue/global-data-protection/)
- **Resource types:** Cross-jurisdictional comparisons, transfer mechanism analyses, adequacy assessments

### Artificial Intelligence
FPF's AI coverage spans the EU AI Act, U.S. federal and state AI regulation, conformity assessment, impact assessment frameworks, and AI governance best practices. The Center for AI produces guides on EU AI Act compliance, prohibited practices analysis, and emerging state AI laws. Approximately 46+ resources covering both regulatory and practical governance dimensions.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/artificial-intelligence/](https://fpf.org/issue/artificial-intelligence/)
- **Key program:** Center for AI
- **Resource types:** Regulatory guides, conformity assessment frameworks, governance best practices, legislative trackers

### Youth and Education Privacy
FPF maintains extensive coverage of children's and student privacy, including COPPA compliance, age-appropriate design codes, student data privacy agreements, and age assurance technologies. The Youth Privacy program operates the Student Privacy Compass, a dedicated K-12 privacy resource hub. Approximately 42+ resources.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/youth-education-privacy/](https://fpf.org/issue/youth-education-privacy/)
- **Key program:** Youth Privacy Program
- **Key tool:** Student Privacy Compass (K-12 privacy resource hub)
- **Resource types:** COPPA guidance, age-appropriate design analysis, student data agreements, children's online safety

### Ad Tech and Digital Advertising
FPF analyzes the privacy dimensions of digital advertising including Universal Opt-Out Mechanisms (UOOMs) — with 7+ states now requiring compliance — behavioral targeting, cross-device tracking, and privacy-protective advertising practices. Resources include the advertising risk-utility framework for evaluating ad tech practices against privacy principles.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/ad-tech/](https://fpf.org/issue/ad-tech/)
- **Key topics:** UOOMs, risk-utility framework, behavioral targeting, Best Practices Repository for advertising
- **Resource types:** Compliance guides, framework analyses, best practice recommendations

### Health Data Privacy
FPF covers privacy dimensions of health data that falls outside traditional HIPAA/FDA regulatory scope, including consumer wearables and fitness trackers, direct-to-consumer genetic testing, LGBTQ+ health privacy, AgeTech, and clinical trial data sharing. Focus areas include emerging health technologies and the gaps in regulatory coverage for consumer health data.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/health-data/](https://fpf.org/issue/health-data/)
- **Key topics:** Consumer wearables, genetic testing privacy, LGBTQ+ health data, health data outside HIPAA scope
- **Resource types:** Policy analyses, gap assessments, best practice guides

### Biometrics and Facial Recognition
FPF has developed a foundational five-type taxonomy for facial recognition technology: (1) detection, (2) characterization, (3) tracking, (4) one-to-one verification, and (5) one-to-many identification. Resources cover biometric privacy principles, state biometric privacy laws (BIPA and similar), and risk-based approaches to biometric technology governance.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/biometrics/](https://fpf.org/issue/biometrics/)
- **Key framework:** Five-type facial recognition taxonomy
- **Resource types:** Taxonomy frameworks, state law analyses, privacy principle guides

### Privacy Enhancing Technologies (PETs)
FPF maintains the PETs Repository, a centralized resource covering differential privacy, fully homomorphic encryption (FHE), federated learning, zero-knowledge proofs (ZKPs), secure multiparty computation, and synthetic data. Resources include technical explainers, model contract clauses for PETs deployment, and real-world implementation case studies.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/pets/](https://fpf.org/issue/pets/)
- **Key tool:** PETs Repository
- **Resource types:** Technical explainers, model contract clauses, implementation case studies

### Immersive Technologies (AR/VR/XR)
FPF analyzes privacy implications of augmented reality, virtual reality, and extended reality technologies. Key contributions include the body-related data risk framework for XR environments, analysis of XR data flows, youth privacy in immersive tech, and privacy considerations for advertising in immersive environments.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/immersive-tech/](https://fpf.org/issue/immersive-tech/)
- **Key framework:** Body-related data risk framework
- **Resource types:** Risk frameworks, data flow analyses, youth privacy guides

### Mobility and Location Data
FPF covers privacy challenges in mobility data including connected vehicles, ride-sharing, transit systems, and location tracking. Key resources include the Mobility Data Sharing Assessment Tool (developed with SAE), analysis of location data fundamentals, heat map re-identification risks, equity and bias concerns in mobility data, and sensitive location redaction approaches.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/mobility-location/](https://fpf.org/issue/mobility-location/)
- **Key tool:** Mobility Data Sharing Assessment Tool (with SAE)
- **Resource types:** Assessment tools, risk analyses, location data guides

### Smart Communities
FPF supports privacy governance in smart city and connected community deployments. Key resources include the Model Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA) Policy for smart communities and the Civic Data Privacy Leaders Network, connecting 30+ local governments working on municipal data privacy. Resources address smart city technology assessment and privacy-by-design for civic infrastructure.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/smart-communities/](https://fpf.org/issue/smart-communities/)
- **Key tool:** Model PIA Policy
- **Key network:** Civic Data Privacy Leaders Network (30+ local governments)
- **Resource types:** Model policies, municipal guidance, technology assessments

### Open Banking and Financial Data
FPF analyzes privacy dimensions of open banking, financial data sharing, and the evolving consumer financial data ecosystem. Coverage includes CFPB guidance on data portability, consent frameworks for financial data, privacy risks in financial data aggregation, and standardization gaps in the emerging data ecosystem.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/open-banking/](https://fpf.org/issue/open-banking/)
- **Resource types:** Ecosystem analyses, consent framework guides, regulatory assessments

### Research Ethics and Data Sharing
FPF advances ethical frameworks for corporate-academic data sharing and research use of personal data. Key resources include the Ethical Data Use Committee (EDUC) framework, the Data Sharing for Research Tracker (directory of organizations sharing data for research, including Google, Meta, Ford, and others), and principles that go "Beyond IRBs" for research data governance.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/research-ethics/](https://fpf.org/issue/research-ethics/)
- **Key program:** Ethical Data Use Committee (EDUC)
- **Key tool:** Data Sharing for Research Tracker
- **Resource types:** Ethical frameworks, data sharing directories, governance principles

### Cybersecurity and Privacy
FPF examines the intersection of cybersecurity and data privacy, including organizational collaboration on security practices, platform privacy requirements from major technology companies (Apple, Google), IoT cybersecurity, and the mutual reinforcement between security measures and privacy protection.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/cybersecurity/](https://fpf.org/issue/cybersecurity/)
- **Resource types:** Platform requirement analyses, IoT security guides, organizational best practices

### Employee Privacy and Workplace
FPF addresses privacy challenges in the modern workplace, including employee monitoring technologies, workplace analytics, remote work surveillance, biometric timekeeping, and the balance between employer legitimate interests and employee privacy expectations. Coverage includes both U.S. and international perspectives on workplace data protection.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/employee-privacy/](https://fpf.org/issue/employee-privacy/)
- **Resource types:** Policy analyses, workplace monitoring guides, international comparisons

### Surveillance and Government Access
FPF analyzes government surveillance authorities, law enforcement access to data, and the legal frameworks governing government requests for personal information. Coverage includes national security letters, mutual legal assistance treaties (MLATs), the CLOUD Act, and international frameworks for cross-border law enforcement data access.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/surveillance/](https://fpf.org/issue/surveillance/)
- **Resource types:** Legal framework analyses, cross-border data access guides, policy recommendations

### De-identification and Anonymization
FPF covers technical and legal approaches to de-identification, anonymization, and pseudonymization of personal data. Resources address re-identification risks, regulatory standards for de-identification across jurisdictions (GDPR vs. CCPA vs. HIPAA approaches), and practical implementation guidance for organizations processing large-scale data.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/de-identification/](https://fpf.org/issue/de-identification/)
- **Resource types:** Technical guides, regulatory comparisons, implementation frameworks

### Consumer IoT and Connected Devices
FPF examines privacy implications of internet-connected consumer devices including smart home products, wearables, connected appliances, and consumer robotics. Coverage addresses data collection transparency, consent mechanisms for always-on devices, data minimization in IoT contexts, and manufacturer privacy obligations.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/consumer-iot/](https://fpf.org/issue/consumer-iot/)
- **Resource types:** Data flow analyses, consent framework guides, manufacturer obligation guides

### Digital Identity
FPF analyzes evolving approaches to digital identity including decentralized identity, digital identity wallets (EU eIDAS 2.0), identity verification technologies, and the privacy implications of different identity architecture choices. Coverage spans government-issued digital IDs, private sector identity solutions, and interoperability standards.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/digital-identity/](https://fpf.org/issue/digital-identity/)
- **Resource types:** Architecture analyses, regulatory guides, interoperability assessments

### Ethics and Responsible Data Use
FPF promotes ethical frameworks for data use that extend beyond legal compliance, covering responsible AI deployment, algorithmic fairness, data ethics review boards, and organizational data governance structures. Resources address the gap between legal compliance and ethical best practice, providing frameworks for organizations seeking to exceed minimum regulatory requirements.
- **Browse:** [https://fpf.org/issue/ethics/](https://fpf.org/issue/ethics/)
- **Resource types:** Ethical frameworks, governance guides, organizational best practices

## 4. FPF Specialized Tools

FPF develops and maintains several interactive tools and trackers that provide ongoing, updated resources beyond static publications:

### Key Dates for State Privacy Laws Tracker
A continuously updated tracker of enforcement dates, cure periods, opt-out signal deadlines, and other critical compliance milestones for U.S. state privacy legislation. Essential for compliance teams managing multi-state privacy programs.
- **Use:** Compliance planning, deadline tracking, enforcement readiness
- **Related issue area:** U.S. Legislation

### Student Privacy Compass
A dedicated K-12 privacy resource hub providing guidance on student data privacy for schools, districts, parents, and education technology companies. Includes compliance resources, best practice guides, and news relevant to the student privacy ecosystem.
- **Website:** [https://studentprivacycompass.org](https://studentprivacycompass.org)
- **Use:** K-12 privacy compliance, FERPA and COPPA guidance, edtech privacy assessment
- **Related issue area:** Youth and Education Privacy

### PETs Repository
A centralized repository of resources on privacy-enhancing technologies including technical explainers, implementation case studies, model contract clauses, and comparative analyses. Covers differential privacy, homomorphic encryption, federated learning, zero-knowledge proofs, secure multiparty computation, and synthetic data.
- **Use:** Technology selection, implementation guidance, contract drafting
- **Related issue area:** Privacy Enhancing Technologies

### Mobility Data Sharing Assessment Tool
Developed in collaboration with SAE International, this tool helps organizations assess the privacy implications of mobility data sharing arrangements. Provides structured evaluation frameworks for connected vehicle data, ride-sharing information, transit data, and location-based service data.
- **Use:** Data sharing agreement evaluation, privacy impact assessment, risk analysis
- **Related issue area:** Mobility and Location Data

### Data Sharing for Research Tracker
A directory of organizations that share data for research purposes, including major technology companies (Google, Meta), automotive manufacturers (Ford), and other data holders. Tracks which organizations participate in data sharing, what data is available, and under what conditions.
- **Use:** Research data access, academic-industry collaboration, data governance reference
- **Related issue area:** Research Ethics and Data Sharing

### Best Practices Repository
A curated collection of codes of conduct, privacy principles, certification programs, and self-regulatory commitments organized by sector and technology area. Provides practitioners with a reference for industry standards and voluntary compliance frameworks.
- **Use:** Benchmarking, self-regulatory compliance, voluntary code adoption
- **Related issue areas:** Multiple (cross-cutting)

## 5. Major FPF Programs

FPF operates several programs that produce specialized content and analysis. These are described here as content-producing entities rather than organizational structures:

### Center for AI
FPF's dedicated AI program focuses on the intersection of artificial intelligence and privacy. Key outputs include:
- EU AI Act compliance and conformity assessment guides
- Analysis of EU AI Act prohibited practices ("red lines")
- U.S. state AI law tracking and analysis (27+ laws across 14+ states)
- AI impact assessment methodology and governance frameworks
- Analysis of federal AI executive orders and their privacy implications

### Youth Privacy Program
FPF's program dedicated to children's and student privacy produces:
- Student Privacy Compass resources and tools
- COPPA compliance guidance and updates
- Age-appropriate design code analysis (UK, California, and emerging frameworks)
- Student data privacy agreement templates and guidance
- Research on children's online safety and age assurance technologies

### Privacy Papers for Policymakers
An annual publication curating the most relevant and significant privacy research for policymakers. Selects papers from academic researchers, think tanks, and practitioners that address active policy questions. Provides a bridge between academic privacy research and the policymaking process.

### DC Privacy Forum
FPF's flagship Washington DC event bringing together privacy professionals, policymakers, industry leaders, and academics. Produces recorded sessions, panel discussions, and keynote presentations that become part of FPF's video and blog content library.

### Training Program
FPF produces educational materials and training content on specific privacy topics, including:
- EU AI Act compliance training
- AI fundamentals for privacy professionals
- De-identification techniques and standards
- Biometric privacy law and technology
- Ad tech privacy and compliance

**Note on URL stability:** Structural URLs (issue area pages, blog index, about pages) are more stable than individual article URLs. When linking to FPF content, prefer issue area pages as navigation starting points over individual publication URLs which may change with site restructuring.

### Content Taxonomy

FPF organizes content primarily by **issue area**. Each publication is tagged to one or more issue areas, and the issue area page serves as the definitive browsing entry point for all related content.

- **Primary axis:** Issue areas (20+ categories listed in Section 3)
- **Secondary axis:** Publication type (Reports, White Papers, Filings, Infographics, Blog Posts, Videos)
- **Cross-references:** Many publications span multiple issue areas (e.g., an AI governance report may appear under both Artificial Intelligence and Global Data Protection)

### Search Strategies

When looking for specific FPF content:

1. **By topic:** Start with the issue area page (`fpf.org/issue/{area-slug}/`). Each area page lists all related publications, tools, and analysis.
2. **By recency:** Check the blog (`fpf.org/blog/`) for the most recent analysis and commentary. Blog posts often announce new reports and publications.
3. **By publication type:** Browse issue area pages and filter mentally by publication type. FPF does not have separate publication-type landing pages, so issue areas are the primary browsing entry point.
4. **By program:** For Center for AI content, start with the Artificial Intelligence issue area. For Youth Privacy content, start with the Youth and Education Privacy issue area.
5. **By tool:** Specialized tools (Key Dates Tracker, Student Privacy Compass, PETs Repository, etc.) are linked from their respective issue area pages and may have dedicated URLs.
