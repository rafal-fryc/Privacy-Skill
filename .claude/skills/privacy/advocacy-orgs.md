# Privacy Advocacy Organizations Catalog

> **Last verified:** March 2026. Organization descriptions, URLs, and resource listings reflect publicly available information as of verification date. Websites and resource offerings may change.

This catalog covers major privacy advocacy organizations -- nonprofits, professional associations, and civil society groups that produce privacy research, policy analysis, tools, and educational resources. These organizations are the primary non-governmental, non-academic sources in the privacy ecosystem.

For government regulators and enforcement agencies, see [gov-regulators.md](gov-regulators.md).
For academic centers and think tanks, see [academic-centers.md](academic-centers.md).
For FPF-specific knowledge, see [fpf-reference.md](fpf-reference.md).

### International Association of Privacy Professionals (IAPP)

**Focus:** The world's largest privacy professional organization, providing certification (CIPP/US, CIPP/E, CIPP/C, CIPP/A, CIPM, CIPT), training, and industry resources for privacy, AI governance, and digital responsibility practitioners. Serves as a central hub connecting the global privacy community.
**Website:** [iapp.org](https://iapp.org)
**Key resources:** Certification programs, enforcement databases, legislation trackers, research articles, web conferences, tools and trackers, Daily Dashboard newsletter
**Notable:** The [Global Privacy and Data Protection Enforcement Database](https://iapp.org/resources/global-privacy-and-data-protection-enforcement-database/) catalogs enforcement actions worldwide with searchable filters. The [US State Privacy Legislation Tracker](https://iapp.org/resources/article/us-state-privacy-legislation-tracker/) monitors comprehensive state privacy bills across all 50 states.

> See [iapp-nav.md](iapp-nav.md) for site navigation guide.

### Electronic Privacy Information Center (EPIC)

**Focus:** Public interest research center focused on civil liberties, privacy, and the First Amendment. EPIC pursues litigation, open government advocacy, and policy research on emerging privacy and technology issues. Known for aggressive FOIA campaigns and amicus briefs in landmark privacy cases.
**Website:** [epic.org](https://epic.org)
**Key resources:** Litigation filings, policy reports, FOIA archives, public voice campaigns, issue-specific resource pages, testimony before Congress
**Notable:** EPIC's [Issue Pages](https://epic.org/issues/) provide deep dives into topics including AI and human rights, consumer privacy, government surveillance, health privacy, and student privacy. EPIC regularly files amicus briefs in Supreme Court and federal appellate privacy cases.

> See [epic-nav.md](epic-nav.md) for site navigation guide.

### Center for Democracy and Technology (CDT)

**Focus:** Nonpartisan nonprofit working to advance civil rights and civil liberties in the digital age. CDT operates from Washington DC, Brussels, and San Francisco, bridging U.S. and EU policy. Covers privacy and data governance, AI governance, free expression, and government surveillance across both advocacy and research dimensions.
**Website:** [cdt.org](https://cdt.org)
**Key resources:** Policy briefs, research reports, legislative analyses, regulatory comments, coalition letters, technical standards contributions
**Notable:** CDT's [Privacy and Data](https://cdt.org/area-of-focus/privacy-data/) work covers consumer privacy legislation, data broker regulation, health privacy, and surveillance. Their EU office provides analysis bridging transatlantic regulatory approaches.

> See [cdt-nav.md](cdt-nav.md) for site navigation guide.

### Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF)

**Focus:** Leading nonprofit defending digital privacy, free speech, and innovation through impact litigation, policy analysis, grassroots activism, and technology development. EFF combines legal advocacy with practical tools that individuals and organizations can deploy to protect their privacy.
**Website:** [eff.org](https://eff.org)
**Key resources:** Impact litigation, legal analyses, activism campaigns, privacy tools, Deeplinks blog, whitepapers on surveillance and encryption
**Notable:** EFF develops and maintains widely used privacy tools including [Privacy Badger](https://privacybadger.org/) (tracker blocker), [Certbot](https://certbot.eff.org/) (HTTPS automation), and [HTTPS Everywhere](https://www.eff.org/https-everywhere). Their [Surveillance Self-Defense](https://ssd.eff.org/) guide provides practical digital security guidance.

### Access Now

**Focus:** International digital rights organization defending and extending the digital rights of users at risk around the world. Operates across policy advocacy, technical assistance, legal intervention, and grassroots organizing with offices in multiple countries. Particularly active on government surveillance, internet shutdowns, and data protection in the Global South.
**Website:** [accessnow.org](https://www.accessnow.org)
**Key resources:** Policy papers, legal interventions, Digital Security Helpline, RightsCon annual conference, advocacy campaigns, country-specific digital rights reports
**Notable:** Access Now's [Digital Security Helpline](https://www.accessnow.org/help/) provides free real-time digital security assistance to civil society, journalists, and activists. [RightsCon](https://www.rightscon.org/) is the world's largest conference on human rights in the digital age, convening stakeholders from 100+ countries.

### Privacy International

**Focus:** UK-based charity challenging government and corporate surveillance worldwide. Conducts investigations, litigation, and advocacy campaigns focused on state surveillance, data exploitation by companies, and the privacy rights of people in the Global South and marginalized communities.
**Website:** [privacyinternational.org](https://privacyinternational.org)
**Key resources:** Investigation reports, litigation filings, long-form data exploitation analyses, campaign resources, country-specific surveillance assessments
**Notable:** Privacy International's [investigations](https://privacyinternational.org/learn) expose surveillance technologies and data exploitation practices globally. Their legal challenges have established important precedents in UK and EU courts regarding bulk surveillance and intelligence sharing.

### Privacy Rights Clearinghouse

**Focus:** U.S. nonprofit empowering individuals to protect their privacy through education, research, and consumer advocacy. Focuses on practical privacy guidance for consumers and maintains one of the longest-running data breach databases in the United States.
**Website:** [privacyrights.org](https://privacyrights.org)
**Key resources:** Consumer privacy fact sheets, data breach database, complaint resources, privacy guides for specific situations (identity theft, medical records, workplace)
**Notable:** The [Data Breach Chronology](https://privacyrights.org/data-breaches) tracked U.S. data breaches since 2005, providing a historical record of breach trends. Their consumer-oriented fact sheets translate complex privacy regulations into practical guidance for individuals.

### American Civil Liberties Union -- Privacy and Technology Project (ACLU)

**Focus:** The ACLU's Privacy and Technology Project litigates, advocates, and educates on privacy and civil liberties issues at the intersection of technology and constitutional rights. Focuses on government surveillance, facial recognition, location tracking, and digital rights through the lens of constitutional law and civil liberties.
**Website:** [aclu.org/issues/privacy-technology](https://www.aclu.org/issues/privacy-technology)
**Key resources:** Litigation case files, policy analyses, state-by-state surveillance legislation guides, public records requests, know-your-rights guides
**Notable:** The ACLU has litigated landmark privacy cases including challenges to NSA mass surveillance, police use of facial recognition, and warrantless location tracking. Their state-level advocacy has driven passage of facial recognition moratoriums and surveillance oversight ordinances in multiple jurisdictions.

### NOYB -- European Center for Digital Rights

**Focus:** Vienna-based privacy enforcement organization founded by Max Schrems, known for strategic GDPR complaints and litigation that have shaped European data protection enforcement. NOYB files targeted complaints with DPAs across EU member states to test and enforce GDPR provisions at scale.
**Website:** [noyb.eu](https://noyb.eu)
**Key resources:** GDPR complaint filings, case tracker, legal analyses, strategic litigation updates, annual reports on GDPR enforcement effectiveness
**Notable:** NOYB's complaints led to the landmark Schrems I and Schrems II CJEU decisions invalidating the EU-US Safe Harbor and Privacy Shield frameworks. Their [complaint tracker](https://noyb.eu/en) documents hundreds of ongoing GDPR complaints filed with DPAs across Europe, targeting cookie consent violations, unlawful data transfers, and access right denials.
